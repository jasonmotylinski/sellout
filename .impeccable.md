## Design Context

### Users
Local community buyers — neighbors, Nextdoor, local Facebook groups — browsing mostly on mobile. Mixed household items: furniture, decor, appliances, clothes. The job: quickly judge whether an item is worth pursuing and reach out. Buyers either know the seller personally or found the link shared locally, so there's inherent trust; the interface just needs to make items look their best.

### Brand Personality
Personal. Curated. Elevated.

The tone is a well-organized estate sale or a thoughtfully curated consignment boutique — not a marketplace, not a garage sale listing app.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Warm editorial boutique.** Cream background (not cold white). Deep espresso text. Cormorant Garamond serif for display type and item names. DM Sans for body, labels, UI. Warm undertones throughout.

Inspired by: EBTH.com (clean, photography-forward, white space), Chairish (warm, curated, serif headings), 1stDibs (editorial restraint). Light mode only.

Anti-references: Facebook Marketplace (cluttered, garish), generic e-commerce card grids, dark mode with neon accents, emoji-heavy UI.

### Design Principles
1. **Photography first.** Items are the stars — UI chrome recedes.
2. **Warmth over sterility.** Cream and serif typography feel personal, not corporate.
3. **Restraint.** Remove rather than add. White space is a design element.
4. **Mobile-native.** Most buyers browse on phones — generous touch targets, readable type.
5. **Elevated without being precious.** Sophisticated enough to make items look good; approachable enough that neighbors trust it.
